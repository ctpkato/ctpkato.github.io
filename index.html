What are Quasi-Linear Convective System tornadoes?

Quasi-Linear Convective System tornadoes, or QLCS for short are tornadoes that spawn without a supercell, as they spawn in a place called a "squall line." A squall line is a line of storms that usually happen at the end of a cold front, which can spawn tornadoes. Although these tornadoes are typically weaker and last shorter on the ground than normal tornadoes, they are still to be respected, as meteorologist don't have a bonafide way to determining whether one will spawn or not. As well as how some of these tornadoes touch down and lift before radar can even detect them, as radars refresh their data every 5 minutes. A good example of this is Tulsa 8/6/2017, where an EF2 tornado touch downed, damaged the city, and lifted before DOPPLER could do anything.
